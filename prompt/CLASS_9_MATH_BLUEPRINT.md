## A. Research Insights

### Learner Pain Points (Bangladesh Class 9 Context)

1. **ভিত্তি দুর্বল (Weak Foundations)**
   - Many Class 9 students arrive without solid command of fractions, decimals, negative numbers, and basic algebraic manipulation from Classes 6-8
   - Students who memorized shortcuts in earlier classes collapse when Class 9 demands conceptual understanding
   - The jump from arithmetic to abstract algebra is the single biggest failure point

2. **পরিভাষা ভীতি (Terminology Fear)**
   - Bangla math terminology (করণী, সূচক, লগারিদম, স্বতঃসিদ্ধ, উপপাদ্য) feels alien
   - Students don't connect Bangla terms to their English equivalents, hurting resource access
   - Many terms appear suddenly without build-up (e.g., "বৈশিষ্ট্যাংশ ও ম্যান্টিসা" with zero context)

3. **Chapter-Specific Difficulty Map**

   | Chapter | Difficulty | Why Students Struggle |
   |---------|-----------|----------------------|
   | বাস্তব সংখ্যা | Medium | Irrational number concept is abstract; surds simplification needs pattern recognition |
   | সেট ও ফাংশন | Medium | Abstract notation; function concept is new and confusing |
   | বর্গ/ঘনসূত্র | Medium-High | Students memorize formulas but can't recognize when to apply which one |
   | উৎপাদকে বিশ্লেষণ | High | The single highest mark-loss topic — students can't identify which method to use |
   | লগারিদম | Very High | Completely new concept; students confuse log rules; mantissa/characteristic feels pointless |
   | দ্বিঘাত সমীকরণ | High | Multiple methods confuse students; discriminant interpretation is weak |
   | জ্যামিতি প্রমাণ | Very High | Students have never written formal proofs; they don't understand what "prove" means |
   | বৃত্তের উপপাদ্য | Very High | Too many theorems; students can't detect which theorem applies to which problem |
   | ত্রিকোণমিতি | High | Blind memorization of ratios without triangle intuition; identity proofs are rote |
   | পরিমিতি | Medium | Formula overload; unit conversion errors; 3D visualization is weak |
   | সহসমীকরণ | Medium | Method selection confusion; graphical solution poorly understood |
   | ধারা | Medium | AP/GP formula mixing; nth term vs sum confusion |

4. **Where Students Lose Marks**
   - Sign errors in algebraic manipulation (especially with negative terms in factorization)
   - Not writing intermediate steps (examiners can't give partial marks)
   - Choosing the wrong factorization method
   - Geometry proofs that are logically incomplete or circular
   - Forgetting to check/verify solutions in equations
   - Trigonometric identity proofs that go in circles instead of LHS→RHS
   - Mensuration unit errors (cm² vs m², missing conversion)
   - Misreading word problems (especially simultaneous equation word problems)

5. **Most Misunderstood Concepts**
   - √(a²) = |a|, not just a (students assume it's always positive a)
   - (a+b)² ≠ a² + b² (the 2ab term is constantly forgotten)
   - log is the INVERSE of exponentiation (students treat it as a formula to memorize)
   - A proof requires logical chain, not just stating the theorem
   - Discriminant tells you about roots WITHOUT solving
   - Tangent is perpendicular to radius AT the point of contact (not "near" it)
   - Mean, median, mode are different measures — each has specific use cases

### Gaps in Existing Free Resources

1. **10 Minute School**: Video-based, solution-focused, but not interactive. No quizzes with spaced repetition. No misconception-targeted teaching.
2. **School Math BD / Education Blog 24**: Solution guides (PDF/blog). No interactivity. Copy textbook solutions without explaining WHY.
3. **NCTB Textbook**: The source material, but dense, assumes strong foundation, no worked examples beyond basics.
4. **Google Play apps**: Solution viewers. No gamification, no SRS, no scaffolding for weak learners.
5. **None** of the existing resources offer:
   - Misconception correction as a teaching strategy
   - Spaced repetition for math formulas/theorems
   - Bug-challenge (find-the-mistake) exercises
   - Scaffolded proof-writing instruction
   - Exam strategy explicitly taught

### Teaching Strategy Recommendations

1. **Theorem lessons must be split**: intuition → statement → proof walkthrough → application drill → mixed detection
2. **Every algebra lesson needs a "common mistake" callout** — students need to see the wrong answer and understand why it's wrong
3. **Geometry needs diagramming instruction** — "draw before you think" must be drilled
4. **Logarithm needs 2-3 lessons of pure exponent review** before introducing log
5. **Trigonometry needs a dedicated "triangle labeling" lesson** before any ratios
6. **Factorization needs a "method selection flowchart" lesson** — the biggest pain point
7. **Word problems need a dedicated "reading math" lesson** — how to extract variables from Bengali text

## CRITICAL RULES — MUST FOLLOW

### Language Rules
1. ALL learner-facing content (title, subtitle, analogy, points, whatIs, realWorld, funFact, quiz, challenge, eli5, codeWalkthrough, bugChallenge) MUST be in natural Bangla
2. Use English terms in parentheses ONLY at first mention of technical terms: "করণী (Surd)", "উৎপাদক (Factor)", "স্পর্শক (Tangent)"
3. Do NOT use machine-translated Bangla. Write like a warm, patient Bangladeshi math teacher talking to a Class 9 student
4. Use "তুমি" (informal you), not "আপনি" — this is peer-friendly teaching
5. Bangla digits (০, ১, ২, ...) in prose text; standard digits (0, 1, 2, ...) in mathematical expressions and code fields

### Math-Specific `code` Field Rules
6. The `code` field is NOT JavaScript. It is a PLAIN TEXT step-by-step worked math solution
7. Format math solutions clearly with line breaks, step numbers, and alignment:
   ```
   সমস্যা: (2x + 3)² বিস্তৃত করো।

   সমাধান:
   (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b² সূত্র ব্যবহার করি
   এখানে a = 2x, b = 3

   ধাপ ১: a² = (2x)² = 4x²
   ধাপ ২: 2ab = 2 × 2x × 3 = 12x
   ধাপ ৩: b² = 3² = 9

   ∴ (2x + 3)² = 4x² + 12x + 9  ✓
   ```
8. `point.code` = a mini worked example (3-6 lines) illustrating that specific point
9. `codeWalkthrough` = array of strings, each explaining ONE line of the main `code` field
10. `bugChallenge.code` = a math solution with EXACTLY ONE realistic mistake that students commonly make

### Content Quality Rules
11. **Analogies**: Must be from Bangladeshi daily life — বাজার করা, ক্রিকেট, রিকশা ভাড়া, জমির মাপ, বিদ্যুৎ বিল, স্কুলের ক্লাস, চা-দোকান, etc. NOT from tech or Western contexts
12. **Points (7-11 per lesson)**: Each teaches ONE concept. Progress simple → complex within the lesson
13. **Quizzes (3-5 per lesson)**: Test UNDERSTANDING, not memorization. All 4 options must be plausible. Include at least one "common mistake" trap option
14. **Bug challenges**: Must contain exactly ONE realistic error (sign error, formula misapplication, skipped step, wrong method choice). The hint guides without revealing. The answer explains WHY the mistake happens
15. **Resources**: Use REAL URLs only. Acceptable sources:
    - Khan Academy (khanacademy.org)
    - 10 Minute School (10minuteschool.com)
    - Math is Fun (mathsisfun.com)
    - GeoGebra (geogebra.org)
    - School Math BD (schoolmathbd.com)
    - YouTube educational channels (3Blue1Brown, Organic Chemistry Tutor, etc.)
    - NCTB official (nctb.gov.bd)
    Do NOT invent URLs. If unsure, use a known working URL from the sources above

### Teaching Philosophy Rules
16. **Zero-assumption teaching**: Never say "as we know" or "obviously". Explain everything from scratch
17. **Every lesson must include at least one misconception callout** — show the WRONG way and explain why it's wrong
18. **Theorem lessons must follow this order**: intuition → formal statement → proof walkthrough → worked application
19. **Geometry lessons**: Always instruct students to "draw a diagram first" — describe what to draw
20. **Trigonometry lessons**: Always reference the actual triangle — never present ratios as abstract formulas
21. **Algebra lessons**: Always explain WHY a manipulation is valid (e.g., "both sides can be divided by the same non-zero number")
22. **Word problems**: Always show the translation step explicitly — "Let x = ..." with clear reasoning
23. **Every lesson should include at least one exam-style problem pattern**

### Difficulty & Progression Rules
24. beginner lessons: simple examples, lots of hand-holding, very small steps
25. intermediate lessons: standard textbook-level problems, some multi-step
26. advanced lessons: exam-level problems, mixed methods, proof construction
27. Within each lesson, points should progress from simple → complex
28. Mixed practice / revision lessons should pull from multiple sub-topics

### Notation Consistency
29. Use Unicode math symbols: ², ³, √, ×, ÷, ±, ≠, ≤, ≥, ∞, π, θ, ∠, ⊥, ∥, △, ∴, ∵
30. Use → for "implies" or "leads to"
31. Use ✓ for correct answers, ✗ for incorrect
32. Fractions: write as a/b in inline, or stacked in code blocks
33. Keep all mathematical expressions in standard (ASCII/Unicode) digits, not Bangla digits

### What NOT to Do
34. Do NOT hallucinate textbook names, fake formulas, or made-up theorems
35. Do NOT use emoji in content (the platform uses SVG icons only)
36. Do NOT wrap the output in markdown code fences — output raw JavaScript
37. Do NOT use "..." or "etc." to skip content — every field must be complete
38. Do NOT make quiz answers always option 0 — distribute correct answers across 0, 1, 2, 3
39. Do NOT repeat the same analogy across multiple lessons
40. Do NOT use overly formal or textbook-style Bangla — write conversationally
